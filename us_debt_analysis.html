Data Analysis of US Debt Situation

An in-depth exploration of the US federal debt landscape

Introduction

This data story is based on an advanced analysis of the US debt situation, conducted using a Kaggle notebook. The analysis utilizes Python and various data analysis libraries to fetch, process, and visualize economic data from the Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) database.

Key Findings

  • Total US federal debt has been rising sharply over time.
  • The debt-to-GDP ratio provides a more nuanced view of the debt situation.
  • Interest payments on the federal debt have significantly escalated since October 2020.
  • In 2023, interest payments per US citizen exceeded $3000.
  • Interest payments now constitute a major portion of the budget deficit.

Conclusions

The analysis reveals the complexities of the US debt landscape, emphasizing the importance of considering both absolute figures and relative metrics to understand the broader economic implications. While the total debt has increased, the debt-to-GDP ratio and interest payments as a fraction of GDP provide a more comprehensive picture of the financial situation.

Methodology

This analysis was conducted using Python, leveraging libraries such as pandas, numpy, matplotlib, and plotly. Data was sourced from the Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) database using the FRED API.
